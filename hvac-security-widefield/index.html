Specialty Service

Mid-Century Ranch Duct Redesign in Security-Widefield

Hundreds of ranch-style homes in Security-Widefield were built between 1955 and 1968 to house families stationed at the former Ent Air Force Base. These mid-century ranches share a common HVAC flaw: undersized 4-inch round ductwork embedded in concrete slabs or routed through shallow crawlspaces. The original builders sized ducts for gravity furnaces — not modern forced-air systems that push 400 CFM per ton of cooling.

The result is predictable: back bedrooms run 8–12°F warmer than the living room in summer and noticeably colder in winter. Homeowners crank the thermostat, the furnace short-cycles, energy bills spike, and comfort never improves. A new furnace installed on old ductwork is money wasted — the bottleneck is the ducts, not the equipment.

Colorado Springs Elite HVAC's duct redesign service replaces undersized runs with properly calculated 6-inch and 8-inch trunk-and-branch layouts. Every joint is sealed with mastic (not tape), and we verify final performance with a duct blaster test to confirm CFM delivery matches Manual D design targets. PPRBD permits are pulled for every redesign. This is the official fix for Security-Widefield's mid-century duct problem.

Permits & Code Compliance

PPRBD Permits for Security-Widefield HVAC Work

Every furnace installation, AC installation, duct redesign, and fuel-oil conversion in Security-Widefield falls under Pikes Peak Regional Building Department (PPRBD) jurisdiction. PPRBD requires mechanical permits for any equipment replacement or new ductwork — and inspects the finished work before sign-off. Unpermitted work can void manufacturer warranties, create insurance complications, and cause problems when you sell.

Colorado Springs Elite HVAC pulls all PPRBD permits as part of every installation project. The permit cost is included in our flat-rate quotes — no hidden fees. We schedule the inspection, meet the inspector on site, and deliver the signed permit card to you. This is the official, code-compliant way to handle HVAC work in Security-Widefield.

If a previous contractor performed unpermitted HVAC work on your home, call (719) 304-5003. We can evaluate the installation, bring it to code, and help you obtain a retroactive permit through PPRBD.

What is fuel-oil to gas furnace conversion?

Fuel-oil conversion replaces an aging oil-fired furnace with a modern natural gas or propane unit. Many older Security-Widefield homes still run on fuel oil, which costs 30–40% more per BTU than natural gas. Conversion includes removing the oil tank, running a new gas line, installing the furnace, and pulling a PPRBD permit.

How does hard water affect HVAC systems?

Security-Widefield's hard water (18–22 grains per gallon) causes mineral scaling inside evaporator coils, humidifier pads, and condensate drain lines. Scale restricts airflow, reduces cooling capacity, and clogs drains — leading to water damage. Annual coil cleaning and humidifier pad replacement prevent these failures.
