Code Compliance

Colorado Springs HVAC Permits & Regulations

All HVAC installations and major replacements in Colorado Springs require permits through the Pikes Peak Regional Building Department (PPRBD). This includes new furnace installations, air conditioner replacements, ductwork modifications, and gas line work.

PPRBD enforces the International Mechanical Code and International Fuel Gas Code as adopted by the State of Colorado. Inspections are required after installation, and work must be performed by a licensed HVAC contractor — which Colorado Springs Elite HVAC is.

As the official local HVAC service for the Colorado Springs area, we handle all permit applications, schedule inspections, and ensure your installation passes PPRBD code requirements the first time. Homeowners who skip permits risk fines, failed inspections during resale, and voided homeowner's insurance coverage.

Do I need a permit for HVAC work in Colorado Springs?

Yes. The Pikes Peak Regional Building Department (PPRBD) requires permits for new HVAC installations, system replacements, ductwork modifications, and gas line work. Colorado Springs Elite HVAC handles all permit applications and inspections as part of our installation service.

How much does AC repair cost vs furnace repair in Colorado Springs?

AC repairs typically range from $150–$650 depending on the issue. Furnace repairs range from $125–$550. We provide upfront, no-surprise quotes before any work begins.

When is the best time to replace an HVAC system in Colorado Springs?

Spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) are ideal for HVAC replacement in Colorado Springs. You'll avoid peak-season demand, get faster scheduling, and often receive manufacturer rebates.

What are the HVAC efficiency requirements in Colorado?

Colorado requires a minimum 14 SEER for central air conditioners and 80% AFUE for gas furnaces. For new construction in El Paso County, higher efficiency standards may apply through PPRBD code compliance.

Yes. We service commercial HVAC systems for offices, restaurants, retail spaces, and warehouses throughout the Colorado Springs area, including downtown and the Powers Corridor business districts.
