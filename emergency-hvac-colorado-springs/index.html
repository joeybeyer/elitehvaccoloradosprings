Know When to Call

What Counts as an HVAC Emergency

Not every HVAC problem requires emergency dispatch, but these five situations warrant an immediate call to (719) 304-5003:

severe_cold

No Heat Below 32°F

When your furnace fails and outside temperatures are below freezing, pipes can burst within hours. Interior temperatures in an unheated Colorado Springs home can drop below 40°F in as little as 4–6 hours during a sub-zero event. This is a life-safety emergency that requires immediate dispatch.

thermostat

No Cool Above 90°F

When indoor temperatures exceed 90°F, heat exhaustion becomes a real risk — especially for elderly residents, infants, and pets. Colorado Springs summer highs regularly hit 90–95°F, and homes with south-facing windows or poor insulation can become dangerously hot within hours of an AC failure.

warning

Gas Smell from Furnace

If you smell rotten eggs or sulfur coming from your furnace or gas line, evacuate the home immediately. Do not flip switches or use electronics. Call 911 first, then Colorado Springs Utilities at (719) 448-4800. Once cleared, call us for emergency gas valve inspection and furnace repair.

detector_smoke

CO Detector Activation

A carbon monoxide detector alarm is a life-threatening emergency. Evacuate everyone immediately, call 911, and do not re-enter until the fire department clears the home. Cracked heat exchangers in furnaces are the leading cause of residential CO leaks in Colorado Springs. Call us after clearance for emergency heat exchanger inspection.

water_damage

Flooding from HVAC Equipment

A clogged condensate drain, cracked evaporator coil pan, or failed condensate pump can dump gallons of water into your home. Active flooding from HVAC equipment causes ceiling, drywall, and flooring damage rapidly. Shut off the system at the thermostat and breaker, then call for emergency dispatch.

No-Heat Calls

Emergency Furnace Repair — No Heat Calls

No-heat calls account for the majority of our winter emergency dispatches in Colorado Springs. When temperatures plummet below zero during a blizzard or chinook wind reversal, furnace failures spike — and every hour without heat matters. Here are the three most common emergency furnace failures we see:

electric_bolt

Ignitor Failure

Hot surface ignitors are the most common furnace failure in Colorado Springs. Symptoms: furnace cycles on, blower runs, but no flame ignites. The system locks out after 3–4 failed ignition attempts. Our technicians carry universal and OEM ignitors on every truck for same-visit replacement.

Typical repair: $185–$285

valve

Gas Valve Failure

Gas valves control fuel flow to the burner assembly. Symptoms: ignitor glows but no gas reaches the burners, or the valve sticks open and the furnace runs continuously. A failed gas valve is both a comfort and safety issue. We stock common Honeywell and White-Rodgers valves for same-day replacement.

Typical repair: $350–$650

crisis_alert

Heat Exchanger Crack

A cracked heat exchanger leaks combustion gases — including carbon monoxide — into your living space. Symptoms: CO detector activation, soot around the furnace, yellow or flickering burner flames, and headaches or nausea when the furnace runs. This requires immediate shutdown and professional inspection.

Typical repair: $800–$2,500

For non-emergency furnace issues — strange noises, reduced airflow, or short-cycling — visit our Furnace Repair Colorado Springs page for standard diagnostic scheduling.

Summer Failures

Emergency AC Repair — Summer Failures

Colorado Springs' high-altitude summer sun and intense UV exposure push air conditioning systems to their limits. At 6,035 feet, lower air density means condenser fans work harder to reject heat, capacitors fail faster, and compressors run at higher pressures. The three most common emergency AC failures we respond to:

sync_problem

Compressor Lockout

The compressor is the heart of your AC system. Lockout occurs when the compressor overheats, detects abnormal pressure, or receives a bad signal from a failing capacitor or contactor. Symptoms: outdoor unit hums but doesn't start, or trips the breaker repeatedly. Our technicians diagnose whether it is a capacitor, contactor, or compressor failure on-site.

Typical repair: $250–$850

ac_unit

Frozen Evaporator Coils

Ice buildup on the indoor evaporator coil blocks airflow and shuts down cooling entirely. Common causes: low refrigerant charge, dirty air filter restricting airflow, or a failing blower motor. Symptoms: warm air from vents, ice visible on refrigerant lines, and water leaking when the ice melts. We thaw, diagnose, and repair the root cause in one visit.

Typical repair: $185–$600

bolt

Electrical Failures

Capacitors, contactors, and wiring connections fail frequently in Colorado Springs due to UV exposure, hailstorm damage, and thermal cycling. Symptoms: clicking sounds from the outdoor unit, AC won't turn on, or intermittent operation. These are usually fast, affordable repairs when caught before they damage the compressor.

Typical repair: $150–$450

For standard AC diagnostics and maintenance, see our AC Repair Colorado Springs page.
