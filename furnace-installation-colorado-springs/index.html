Altitude-Rated Engineering

How We Size a Furnace at 6,000+ ft

At Colorado Springs' elevation of 6,035 ft, gas furnaces experience significant altitude derating — approximately 4% capacity loss per 1,000 ft above sea level. A furnace rated at 100,000 BTU at sea level delivers only about 76,000 BTU in Colorado Springs. Installing without accounting for this derating leads to undersized or oversized systems that waste energy, short-cycle, and fail prematurely.

Our Manual J load calculation process accounts for every variable that affects heating demand in Colorado Springs:

landscape

Altitude Derating

4% BTU loss per 1,000 ft — roughly 24% total at our elevation. We calculate actual delivered BTU, not nameplate ratings.

air

Chinook Wind Exposure

Homes on the west side face 60+ mph chinook gusts. Windward wall exposure increases heat loss and requires higher BTU capacity.

thermostat

Temperature Extremes

Colorado Springs sees a 140°F seasonal swing — from -20°F winter lows to 100°F+ summer highs. Design temperature is critical.

home

Insulation & Envelope

R-values, window U-factors, air infiltration rates, and duct leakage all factor into the Manual J calculation for correct sizing.

The risk of oversizing: An oversized furnace heats your home too quickly, short-cycling on and off before completing a full heating cycle. This wastes gas, increases humidity swings, accelerates wear on the heat exchanger and blower motor, and shortens the system's lifespan by years. Proper Manual J sizing at altitude prevents every one of these problems.

Save on Your Installation

Colorado Springs Utilities Rebates

Colorado Springs Utilities (CSU) offers rebate programs for homeowners who install qualifying high-efficiency furnaces. Rebate amounts and eligibility change by program year, but the following programs have historically been available to Colorado Springs residents:

High-Efficiency Gas Furnace Rebate

Furnaces rated 95% AFUE or higher may qualify for CSU rebates. Previous program years have offered $200–$400 per qualifying unit. ECM (electronically commutated motor) blowers often qualify for additional incentives.

Federal Energy Tax Credits

The Inflation Reduction Act provides federal tax credits for qualifying high-efficiency HVAC equipment. Gas furnaces meeting the Energy Star Most Efficient criteria (97%+ AFUE) may qualify for up to $600 in federal tax credits.

Trane

Furnace Installation FAQ — Colorado Springs

How much does furnace installation cost in Colorado Springs?

How do you size a furnace for Colorado Springs altitude?

We perform a Manual J load calculation adjusted for Colorado Springs' 6,035 ft elevation. Gas furnaces lose approximately 4% capacity per 1,000 ft above sea level — roughly 24% total derating at our altitude. We factor in BTU requirements, insulation R-values, window orientation, chinook wind exposure, and the 140°F seasonal temperature swing to determine the correct furnace size.
