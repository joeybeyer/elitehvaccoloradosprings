Rooftop Unit Specialists

Commercial RTU Service & Replacement

Rooftop units are the backbone of commercial HVAC in Colorado Springs. These packaged systems combine heating, cooling, and ventilation in a single cabinet mounted on your building's roof — and they take a beating from Colorado's hail, UV exposure, and extreme temperature swings. Colorado Springs Elite HVAC services all major RTU brands from 2-ton single-zone units to 25-ton multi-stage packaged systems.

Our RTU service includes economizer calibration to maximize free cooling during Colorado Springs' mild spring and fall shoulder seasons, curb adapter fabrication and replacement when swapping units of different footprints, gas heat section inspection and burner cleaning, and complete electrical diagnostics on 3-phase power systems. We stock common RTU components — contactors, capacitors, fan motors, belts, and control boards — for same-day repairs that minimize your business downtime.

When replacement is more cost-effective than repair, we handle the full scope: engineering the curb adapter transition, crane coordination for rooftop placement, PPRBD commercial permit pulls, refrigerant line modifications, and thermostat or building automation system integration. Every RTU replacement includes a post-install inspection and commissioning to verify airflow, refrigerant charge, and gas pressures at altitude.

Know the Difference

Commercial vs Residential — What's Different

Business owners often ask why commercial HVAC costs more and requires specialized technicians. The differences are significant and affect everything from equipment selection to permitting requirements. Understanding these distinctions helps you make informed decisions about your commercial HVAC investment.

Tonnage and capacity: Residential systems typically range from 2–5 tons. Commercial systems start at 5 tons and scale to 25+ tons for large retail and warehouse spaces. Higher tonnage means larger compressors, more refrigerant, heavier electrical loads, and structural considerations for rooftop mounting.

3-phase power: Most commercial buildings in Colorado Springs run 3-phase electrical service. Commercial HVAC equipment uses 3-phase motors that are more efficient at higher loads but require different wiring, contactors, and protection devices than single-phase residential systems. Technicians need specific training and equipment to diagnose 3-phase electrical issues.

Building codes and permits: PPRBD commercial permits carry stricter requirements than residential permits. Commercial HVAC installations must comply with International Mechanical Code (IMC) commercial provisions including ventilation rates per occupancy type, fire damper requirements, and energy code calculations. Colorado Springs Elite HVAC handles all PPRBD commercial permit applications and inspections.

Zone control: A typical home has one or two zones. A commercial building may have 10–20+ zones controlled by VAV boxes, programmable thermostats, or a building automation system. Proper zone control keeps individual tenants comfortable while optimizing energy use across the entire building — a balancing act that requires commercial-specific expertise.

How much does commercial HVAC service cost in Colorado Springs?

Commercial HVAC service calls in Colorado Springs start at $129 for diagnostics (waived with approved repair). Hourly rates for commercial work run $145–$195 depending on system complexity. How is commercial HVAC different from residential?

Commercial HVAC systems operate at higher tonnage (5–25+ tons vs. 2–5 residential), often use 3-phase power instead of single-phase, require zone control for multiple occupied spaces, and must meet stricter building codes including PPRBD commercial permits. Rooftop packaged units, make-up air systems, and economizers are common in commercial but rare in residential.
