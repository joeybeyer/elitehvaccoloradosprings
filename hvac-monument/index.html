Monument HVAC FAQ

Do altitude-related issues affect my furnace in Monument?

Yes. At 7,400 feet, Monument's thin air reduces oxygen availability, which affects gas combustion in furnaces. Orifices may need to be derated, and pressure switches calibrated for high altitude. Our technicians specialize in altitude-specific furnace adjustments to ensure safe, efficient operation.

Do I need an HVAC permit in Monument?

Yes. Monument falls under the Pikes Peak Regional Building Department (PPRBD) jurisdiction. All HVAC installations, replacements, and gas line work require permits and inspections. We handle the entire process for you.

How much does HVAC installation cost in Monument?

A complete HVAC system installation in Monument typically ranges from $6,000 to $18,000 depending on home size, system type, and ductwork requirements. High-altitude derating may add modest cost. We provide free in-home estimates.

Do you offer emergency HVAC service in Monument?

Yes. We provide 24/7 emergency HVAC repair throughout Monument and northern El Paso County. No overtime charges. Call (719) 304-5003 anytime.

What's the best HVAC system for Monument's climate?

Given Monument's 7,400-foot elevation and cold winters, we recommend high-efficiency gas furnaces (95%+ AFUE) paired with central AC or heat pumps. Dual-fuel systems are also popular for their efficiency in this climate.

How often should I service my HVAC system in Monument?

Twice a year — a spring AC tune-up and a fall furnace inspection. Monument's altitude and extreme winter temperatures make regular maintenance especially important for safe, efficient operation.
